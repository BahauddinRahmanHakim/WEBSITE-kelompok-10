AK-47
The AK-47, or Avtomat Kalashnikova 1947, is a gas-operated, selective-fire assault rifle developed in the Soviet Union by Mikhail Kalashnikov. Renowned for its durability, simplicity, and low production cost, the AK-47 features a 7.62x39mm cartridge, making it effective in various combat situations. Its iconic curved magazine typically holds 30 rounds, and it has become one of the most widely used firearms in the world.

AXMC
The AXMC (Accuracy International Multi-Caliber) is a modular bolt-action sniper rifle known for its precision and versatility. Designed for military and law enforcement use, it can be easily converted between different calibers, including .338 Lapua Magnum and .308 Winchester. The AXMC features a robust chassis, adjustable stock, and high-quality optics, making it a preferred choice for long-range engagements.

MAKAROV PISTOL
The Makarov pistol is a semi-automatic handgun developed in the Soviet Union in the late 1940s. Chambered primarily in 9x18mm Makarov, it is compact and lightweight, making it suitable for concealed carry. The Makarov is known for its reliability and ease of use, featuring a simple blowback design and a single-stack magazine that typically holds 8 rounds. It has been widely used by military and police forces across Eastern Europe.

FN SCAR
The FN SCAR (Special Operations Forces Combat Assault Rifle) is a modular rifle designed by FN Herstal for U.S. Special Operations Forces. Available in two primary variants—the SCAR-L (5.56x45mm) and SCAR-H (7.62x51mm)—it features a short-stroke gas piston system for improved reliability. The SCAR is praised for its accuracy, adaptability, and ruggedness, and can be easily customized with various accessories, making it a favored choice among military and tactical users.
